CV / resume


I am an assistant professor of computer science at Cornell. This is my personal site, and I also have an academic one.

At Cornell, I research numerical methods and algorithmic frameworks that enable new, better, and bigger analyses of complex data. This research has been recognized with a KDD best paper award, a JP Morgan Chase AI Faculty Award, and the Gene Golub Doctoral Dissertation Award, and has been supported with grants from the Army Research Office and the National Science Foundation. This research has led to publications in journals such as Science, Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, and SIAM Review, and at the leading machine learning and data mining conferences, including Neurips, ICML, ICLR, KDD, and WWW. I also teach classes on data science, network science, matrix computations, and numerical optimization.

Before Cornell, I spent nine years in the Bay Area, where I completed my PhD and MS at Stanford University and BS and BA at the University of California, Berkeley. During summers, I interned at Google (four times), Sandia National Laboratories, and HP Labs.
